Passions and Values

With regards to software engineering I have a deep passion for developing complex systems to the highest standards. This includes the application of best practices in software engineering like the SOLID design principles, reasoned architectures and well-directed testing strategies including unit and integration tests.

A key factor in software engineering are the tools one uses. Famously, 70% of security bugs in Google Chrome were once attributed to unsafe memory handling in C and C++ ( source ). This goes to show, that even some of the most skilled engineers made a lot of mistakes because the language they used permitted it. I want my tools, especially my programming languages, to prevent me from introducing as many bugs as possible. Therefore I am a strong supporter of strong, static and expressive type systems as these prevent the introduction of bugs and even tend to speed up development in sufficiently complex codebases. This is why I really admire languages like Rust and Golang.

Furthermore I think that professional software engineering requires at least as many communication skills as it requires technical known how. The best engineers do not just write code for the computer to understand, but for people as well. These people might be their co-workers or even themselves after some time has passed. During my past experiences I have seen some codebases which worked functionally but turned out overly complex and elaborated while trying to optimize for performance and/or adaptability. In the end, almost none of these optimizations were really necessary and the same result could be achieved with only a fraction of the complexity. While to some degree reasonable, excessive and premature optimizations almost always cause more harm than they prevent. This is why I seek to keep complexity down if there is no compelling reason for it and spend considerable time to find elegant and simple solutions as this investment will pay of in the long term.
